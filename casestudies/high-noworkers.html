No Workers?

Sat, Feb 5, 2022

Read in 2 minutes

No workers? A Sr. Labor Economist Ron Hetrick takes a look from ten thousand feet.

You see help wanted signs everywhere. It might be easy to blame COVID, but it’s not that simple. Please watch https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=w05QgHwq8Ig where Ron Hetrick takes a ten-thousand foot look.

To summarize some of his key points: - The baby boomers’ are leaving the job market faster than our society can replace them. We should have a replacement rate of around 2.1, but right now, we are at 1.7. - Our immigration has dropped to zero whereas previously we were adding about one-half a million people per year to our economy. - Workers feel they can quit and find a better paying job. They can and they are.

Ron also notes some things employers can do to find the workers they need. He divides them up into short-term and long-term.

Short Term

  • Wages - Well yes and no. In the longer term, these aren’t as powerful a motivator as other factors.
  • Non-wage passion for the workplace is important. The French phrase says it best: you’ve got to have esprit de corp.

  • Simplify job descriptions to make hiring easier and offer in-house career training for your workers.

    Long Term

  • Workforce planning should be done starting at the board level then the executive suite.

  • Promote good people

  • Don’t let good people quit. If they want flex hours, you can do that easier than hiring another person.

  • Partner with other organizations should a schools where you can interest students in your company.
